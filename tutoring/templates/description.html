How this works

{% if session["isTutor"] %}

Tutor account instructions:

Use the navigation at the top of the page to see your subjects and add subject(s) you would like to tutor in
You can pick an existing subject from the list or add a new subject not on the list.

When a user requests tutoring in a certain subject on your list, they may be allocated to you.
Details of any users allocated to you will show up in a list on your homepage
From there, you can contact your tutees individually and arrange a breaktime for tutoring.
{% else %}
Use the navigation at the top of the page to reserve an allocation for a certain subject you would like tutoring in
Choose a subject from the list and pick your year group
Submit the request and you'll immediately be allocated a tutor from our team for that subject

Details of your tutor(s) will show up in a list on your homepage
From there, you can contact a certain tutor and arrange a breaktime for tutoring
Your tutor(s) will also be able contact you when you're allocated to them

If you would like tutoring in a subject not on the list, you can request a new a subject from the same page
After approval from admin, you may be allocated a tutor for that subject
{% endif %} {% if session["isAdmin"] %}

Admin account instructions:

As admin, you have access to all of the user data, allocation data and tutor data
You can find these on the admin page at the navigation bar at the top

You can contact users individually and edit user account details in case someone has forgotten their login details or for other similar reasons
Non-admin users cannot edit their account name or email and cannot access data about other users
You can see details of the people involved in each allocation, the number of allocations a certain tutor has, and the subjects a tutor is offering

Be cautious when editing user details, as changes to a user account type between tutor and tutee may cause the deletion of allocations
Changes to user details or allocations cannot be undone once submitted
{% endif %} {% endblock %}
